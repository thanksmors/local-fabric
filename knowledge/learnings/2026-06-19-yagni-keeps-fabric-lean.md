# Learning

Fabric works best when it combines minimal always-present navigation seams with strict gates for real content.

# Applies To

- Creating child `AGENTS.md` files.
- Creating ICM `stages/` and stage `CONTEXT.md` files.
- Creating OKF concepts, research notes, decision records, and learning notes.
- Adding scripts, templates, configuration, frameworks, abstractions, or local conventions.

# Why It Matters

Without YAGNI, agents may treat Fabric templates as permission to generate scaffolding. With YAGNI, the base stays useful: minimal now, linked always, durable when earned.

# Evidence

- [YAGNI Manifesto](../research/2026-06-19-yagni-manifesto.md)
- [YAGNI Gate decision](../decisions/2026-06-19-yagni-gate.md)

# Reuse Guidance

Before creating context structure, ask who needs it today and whether a smaller link, row, note, or local convention solves the current task. Record exceptions only when the tradeoff is durable.
